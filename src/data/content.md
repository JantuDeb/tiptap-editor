**Solution:**

Given:

Distance between the foot of the tower and point of observation = BC =
20 m

Angle of elevation of the top of the tower = θ = 60°

And, Height of tower = AB = h m

Now, from fig. ABC

$$\tan\theta = \frac{Opposite\ side\ (AB)}{Adjacent\ side\ (BC)}$$

$$\Rightarrow h = 20\tan{60{^\circ}}$$

$$\Rightarrow h = 20 \times \sqrt{3}$$

$\Rightarrow h = 20\sqrt{3}$ m

Hence height of tower is $20\sqrt{3}$ meters.
